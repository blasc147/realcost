{% load crispy_forms_tags %} {% load humanize %} The Real Cost of Car Loans
Real Cost compares dealership loans versus bank loans. What we don't usually realize as consumers is that new car dealerships always give rebates for using cash that you wont get if using a dealership loan.
When determrining who to loan from you should be adding the reabte value to the total cost of interest from the dealership loan. In some cases you can get a big enough cash rebate that a bank loan may be the better option. This app aims to help you make a more informed decison as a consumer.

Forumla used for monthly payment and interest is based on ammortization where the interest depreciates after every payment. For more information about ammortization see the wikipedia entry.
Note: On dealership calculations the rebate it used to calculate the true cost/interest of the dealership loan.
TIP: When you are getting a loan make sure it has the following three things:
  1. The interest depreciates after every payment
  2. The interest rate is fixed throughout the the life of the loan
  3. You are allowed to pay off the full ammount of the loan anytime without penalty
